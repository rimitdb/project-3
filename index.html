Frequently Asked Questions

Many people confuse event planning with event design. Event design can mean many things. Event planning entails various aspects of an event. What is the difference? Event planning is a fast-paced, high energy occupation that appeals to many. This industry has grown exponentially in the past decade. What does an event planner do and how does that differ from an event designer? Event planning differs from event design due to its extensive responsibility. Event design encompasses the more creative side of event management. While a planner focuses on organization, we focus on the decor of the event. From draping to lighting, furniture, and centerpieces, it is our job to ensure your event is truly stunning.

Planning an event can take from weeks to months, it really depends on the size and the details that the event involves. Having enough time allows you to comfortably cover different areas and prepare yourself for potential scenarios that might happen so you can consider various options prior the date. You always need more time than you think! With our experience, planning private and corporate events requires a shorter period of time because guests usually have already confirmed their attendance and no details are needed to be released to the public. The only things that need planning are the event elements such as catering, entertainment, technical and venue.

Our cancellation and refund policies: 1. Due to limited seating, we request that you cancel at least 7 days before a scheduled event. If you have to cancel your event, we offer you a credit to your account if you cancel before the 7 days. 2. Cancellations made within 3 - 6 days will incur a 20% fee. Cancellations made within 72 hours to the event will incur a 30% fee.

All events must have a budget. It’s the glue that keeps everything together. It tells us what is affordable on a small or sometimes large scale, yet we tend to want more than we are allowed. Having a budget plan is the most effective way for all event managers to stay on top of their budget. Keeping track of your budget is not an easy task, but one that is an absolute must to keep your event calculated and stress-free. We follow 3 simple steps as followings: 1) Review Your Expenses. 2) Set Goals and Measure ROI. 3) Create a Plan of Action.
